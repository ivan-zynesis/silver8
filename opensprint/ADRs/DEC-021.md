## Question

When/how does Kafka-style partitioned messaging enter our architecture?

## Operator Decision

**It doesn't, unless we adopt Kafka itself.** We will not hand-roll partitioned topics, consumer groups, partition routing, or ordering guarantees inside our Bus implementation. If a future requirement demands those properties (durable replay, parallel partitioned consumption, strict per-key ordering across replicas), we adopt Kafka.

Symbol sharding for the ingestion tier (DEC-017) is **operational placement** — "which node owns the upstream WS for BTC-USD" — and is explicitly *not* a partition protocol. It's a routing table; closer to consistent-hashing-of-symbols-to-nodes than to Kafka partitions.

## Consideration Factors

- **Use NATS / Redis pub-sub for fanout, Kafka for partitioned/durable** — clear separation: fanout is a "dumb pipe"; durable + partitioned is its own concern with its own well-built solution.
- **Build partition routing inside our Bus** — would slowly grow into a worse, less battle-tested Kafka. Classic anti-pattern. Two engineers spend a quarter on it; a third quarter on its bugs.
- **Use Kafka for fanout too** — would work; overkill for fanout that doesn't need durability. NATS or Redis pub-sub is the right tool for hot fanout.

## Rationale

This is a *strategic* commitment: a future maintainer who finds themselves implementing partition routing in this codebase should stop and adopt Kafka instead. The ADR exists to be discoverable when that temptation arises.

The Bus interface (DEC-005) is intentionally lossy and unordered-across-topics for exactly this reason. An adapter author who tries to add ordering or durability is changing the contract; it should be a deliberate, documented decision.

## Invalidation Trigger

Revisit if:
- A consumer requirement appears that genuinely needs partition routing or durable replay — the answer is "adopt Kafka," not "extend Bus."
- A platform emerges that gives us partitioned + durable + simple in one box (Cloudflare Durable Objects + Queues, NATS JetStream).
