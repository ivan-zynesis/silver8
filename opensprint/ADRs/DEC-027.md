## Question

When does the hub open and close its connection to the upstream venue? In v1 we shipped pre-subscribed-on-startup ("always-on") and explicitly deferred demand-driven semantics — when do we fulfill that?

## Operator Decision

Convert ingestion to **demand-driven with tiered grace periods**. This is the production-target behavior, not a configurable mode:

- **Channel-level**: subscribe upstream Coinbase channel when the first consumer subscribes to a topic; unsubscribe the channel immediately when the last consumer leaves the topic. Driven by `Registry.onDemandChange` (the primitive established in DEC-005 and DEC-006).
- **Socket-level**: when zero channels are subscribed, keep the WS socket warm for an idle window. Close it after **5 minutes** of zero-channel idleness (configurable via env). On next subscribe, reconnect.

The two tiers serve different timescales: channel unsub is the cheap, frequent operation; socket close is the expensive operation reserved for genuinely idle hubs.

## Consideration Factors

- **Demand-driven, tiered grace (chosen)** — matches the production mental model; observable lifecycle transitions for the dashboard demo (DEC-025); efficient under churn (channel ops are cheap, sockets stay warm).
- **Always-on (what v1 shipped)** — simplest; warms books for demo; but defeats the refcount-as-correctness promise (DEC-017) — we hold upstream capacity even when no consumers want it. Rejected as the long-term shape.
- **Configurable mode (`eager` | `demand_driven`)** — both code paths to maintain; doubles the surface area of integration tests; operator framing was explicit that we *commit* to demand-driven as the production target rather than carry both forever.
- **Aggressive close (no grace)** — drop socket the instant demand=0. Causes thrash on consumer churn; reconnect + snapshot-rebuild costs accumulate; defeats the "watch a clean transition" demo because immediate disconnect/reconnect storms look like flapping.
- **Pure channel-level** (no socket-level idle close) — socket stays open forever; cheap on a singleton ingestion node but doesn't fulfill the operator's articulated test flow ("hub closes connection to coinbase").

## Rationale

The operator framed the methodology as "always-on first → observe → convert → mock → automate." The convert step is this ADR. Tiered grace makes the conversion clean rather than chaotic:

- Channel ops happen on every consumer connect/disconnect — they need to be cheap and immediate.
- Socket ops happen on a much slower timescale (genuine idle), so a longer grace is appropriate. Five minutes is large enough that consumer reconnection scenarios don't trigger it, small enough that operators can demonstrate the close in a normal walkthrough.

Coinbase's reconnect time is ~1 second; channel resubscribe + snapshot is similar. Reconnecting on demand after 5 minutes idle costs ~2 seconds of latency for the first consumer of the new wave — acceptable for the savings in held upstream capacity.

This ADR fulfills the deferred-action note in DEC-007 ("Demand-driven dynamic upstream subscription via Registry.onDemandChange — primitive present, action deferred"). DEC-007 itself remains active for the venue-adapter and normalized-format aspects.

## Invalidation Trigger

- Five-minute default proves wrong in practice — either too aggressive (surprising disconnects during natural lulls) or too slow (visible idle waste). Configurable so easy to tune.
- A consumer class emerges that needs guaranteed-connected-upstream regardless of demand (e.g. always-on recorder for compliance). Could reintroduce a configurable always-on mode for those deployments.
- Coinbase rate-limits reconnects in a way that makes the close-then-reopen cost worse than holding the socket. Would tune the idle window up or revisit the close decision.
