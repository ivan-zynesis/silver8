## Question

How is the `CoinbaseAdapter`'s catalog (DEC-030) populated in v1?

## Operator Decision

A small, hardcoded list of common pairs (BTC-USD, ETH-USD, SOL-USD, AVAX-USD, plus a few that round out the demo-friendly set). The list lives in the `CoinbaseAdapter` source as a typed constant; it is not user-configurable in v1.

Coinbase REST `GET /products` discovery is named explicitly as the obvious upgrade — it would always reflect the venue's actual product list, hydrate per-pair metadata for `describe_topic`, and decouple the catalog from code changes — but is **deferred**. The decision to ship REST discovery, when it lands, is its own ADR (with its own consideration factors around startup-readiness behavior, rate posture, and metadata caching).

## Consideration Factors

- **Hardcoded common pairs (chosen)** — passes the take-home assessment; no new dependency; readable; bounded cardinality keeps dashboard UX clean.
- **REST discovery at startup** — always current; but adds a new client (REST), a new failure mode (REST down at startup → readiness behavior question), and Coinbase's full product catalog is hundreds of items most of which we don't want surfaced. Without an allowlist it produces a worse UX than the hardcoded set.
- **Hybrid: hardcoded allowlist + REST hydrates per-pair metadata** — the production-shaped answer. Right shape, wrong moment — adds REST plumbing the brief does not reward.
- **Env-var allowlist (`AVAILABLE_SYMBOLS=...`)** — still hardcoded in essence, just relocated to deployment config. Fine, but the operator's stance was that a few common pairs is enough; a configurable allowlist is gratuitous for v1.

## Rationale

The point of separating this ADR from DEC-030 is to draw a clear line: **the structural commitment (DEC-030) is the architecturally correct part; the operational source (DEC-031) is pragmatic and can change without disturbing DEC-030.** Hardcoded today, REST tomorrow, REST + allowlist after that — the consumers of the catalog (status payload, `list_topics`, dashboard, helpful errors) do not notice. That separation is exactly what the seam buys us.

A future Binance adapter is free to make a different choice — Binance's product list might be small enough to discover, or large enough to require allowlisting. The catalog-source-of-truth decision is per-adapter, not per-system.

## Invalidation Trigger

Revisit if:
- The hardcoded set drifts noticeably from Coinbase's listed products in a way that confuses agents or operators (e.g. a pair we list gets delisted; a popular new pair gets added). Resolution: ship REST discovery (the deferred path) — that's its own ADR.
- Operator UX needs per-deployment configurability (different pairs in different environments). Resolution: env-var allowlist as an intermediate step before REST.
- A second venue lands and the asymmetry in catalog-source choices becomes confusing — could suggest pulling some discovery logic up into shared utility code, but the per-adapter decision stays.
